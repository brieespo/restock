How To Use

One catalog, four statuses

Every product you use and rebuy lives here — with its brand, where to buy it, and the price. Status is one tap from the card: Stocked → Running low → Out → Ordered → back to Stocked. Standing in the bathroom noticing the lotion's almost gone? One tap and future-you remembers.

Category tabs & search

Tabs split the catalog by category; search covers names, brands, stores, and notes. The ⚠️ Needs attention filter shows everything that's low, out, or on order.

Paste a list

The fastest way in: ⋯ More → Paste a list, one product per line as "Brand – Product – Store" (a plain product name works too). Import, then fix details on any card whenever.

Discontinued watch

Flag a product 🚨 when it's being phased out — a reminder to stock up whenever you see it.

Bought it! & the rebuy prediction

Tap 🛒 Bought it! any time you restock something — it logs today's date (editable), prefills the last price/store, and marks the product Stocked. Once a product has 2+ logged purchases, Restock learns your real rebuy pace (the median gap between purchases) and predicts when you'll need it again — no manual guessing. With just one purchase, set a rough "rebuy every ~N days" estimate on the product's edit screen until the real pattern emerges.

Predicted due soon

Products nearing their predicted rebuy date surface in their own 🔮 Predicted due soon panel — even if you never marked them low. This is a separate signal from status: "you marked this low" and "you usually rebuy around now" are shown distinctly. Tap 🛒 Bought it! if it's time, or 😌 Still fine, +2wk to snooze it — snoozing gently widens the prediction so the radar gets quieter and more accurate the more you use it. Marking something Out earlier than predicted does the opposite, tightening the estimate.

Shopping mode

The 🛒 Shopping tab groups everything you currently need — running low, out, on order, or due soon — by store, checklist style, with an estimated total per store. Check items off as you grab them, then tap ✓ Mark checked as bought to log all of them as purchased at once (today's date, that store, listed price) — the fastest way to feed the interval engine.

Restock rhythm

The heat-strip at the top of Shopping mode previews predicted run-outs over the next 8 weeks — a quiet week or two, then a cluster lighting up together, is your "big Costco run" signal. Tap a week to see exactly what's predicted to run out then.

Price watch & shrinkflation

Add a size when logging a purchase (e.g. "19 oz") and Restock tracks price-per-unit over time. Once the same size has been bought at meaningfully different times, a noticeable jump (8%+) surfaces as a 📈 Price watch panel on Home and a chip on the product — the kind of creeping cost nobody notices without the data.
